# Society 5.0 Subline — Economic Foundations

This subline develops the institutional, policy, and welfare implications of Cognitive Factor Economics for Society 5.0 transitions in developing economies.

## Research Agenda

### S5.0-A: The Economic Definition of Human-Centricity
- **Question:** What does "human-centric AI" mean in production function terms?
- **Answer (from CFE):** Maximize investment in augmentable human capital ($H^A$), not merely minimize displacement of routine cognitive capital ($H^C$)
- **Target:** Policy brief for OECD/CEPAL

### S5.0-B: Institutional Requirements for Society 5.0 Transitions
- **Question:** What institutional threshold must a region cross to achieve cognitive augmentation clusters?
- **Components:** Data governance, educational system alignment, labor market flexibility, AI access policy
- **Linked to:** Paper 3 (Geography of Cognitive Augmentation)
- **Target:** World Development

### S5.0-C: Society 5.0 Welfare Criterion
- **Question:** How to evaluate whether a country is progressing toward Society 5.0?
- **Criterion:** Augmentation inequality decreasing while average augmentation capability increases
- **Linked to:** Paper 4 (Distributive Justice)
- **Target:** Economic Policy

### S5.0-D: Colombia's Path to Society 5.0 — CONPES 4144 Evaluation
- **Question:** Does Colombia's National AI Policy (2025, $479B COP) target $H^A$ development or $H^C$ protection?
- **Method:** Apply AHC framework to evaluate policy alignment with Society 5.0 goals
- **Data:** CONPES 4144 text, budget allocations, MinTIC programs
- **Target:** CEPAL Review / Desarrollo y Sociedad

### S5.0-E: Society 5.0 in the Global South — Informality as Structural Constraint
- **Question:** How does labor market informality (40-70% in LatAm) alter the transition dynamics to Society 5.0?
- **Mechanism:** Informal workers are shielded from displacement but excluded from augmentation — a welfare trap
- **Cross-cuts:** Papers 1, 3, 4
- **Target:** World Development / Journal of Development Economics

### S5.0-F: Educational System Redesign for Augmentable Human Capital
- **Question:** What curriculum changes maximize $H^A$ formation?
- **Shift:** From routine-cognitive skills (data entry, standard coding, rote analysis) to augmentable skills (contextual judgment, cross-domain synthesis, ethical reasoning, collaborative intelligence)
- **Application:** Framework for Colombian universities (EAFIT, ESUMER, UdeA)
- **Target:** Economics of Education Review / Policy paper
